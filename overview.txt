🩺 Project Title: Open-Scope: The Intelligent Edge-AI Stethoscope
Tagline: Empowering Rural Healthcare with Offline Acoustic Triage
1. The Problem Statement
The Gap: Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are a leading cause of death in rural India. However, Primary Health Centers (PHCs) lack trained cardiologists.
The Barrier: Traditional stethoscopes rely entirely on the listener's expertise. A rural health worker (ASHA/ANM) can hear a heartbeat but often lacks the training to distinguish between benign sounds and pathological murmurs (e.g., Mitral Regurgitation).
The Failure of Existing Tech: "Digital Stethoscopes" exist but are either prohibitively expensive (>₹20,000) or require high-speed internet to send audio to a cloud server—infrastructure that is unreliable in remote villages.
2. The Proposed Solution
Open-Scope is a low-cost, open-source digital stethoscope that performs on-device diagnosis.
Instead of just amplifying sound, it uses TinyML (Edge AI) to classify heart sounds as "Normal" or "Abnormal" locally on the microcontroller, without needing a smartphone or internet connection.
3. Technical Architecture (The "ECE" Core)
A. Analog Front-End (The Sensor Stage)
Sensor: Piezoelectric diaphragm (Contact Mic) to capture chest vibrations directly, naturally rejecting 80% of ambient room noise.
Signal Conditioning:
Impedance Buffer: A JFET-input Op-Amp (TL072) buffer stage to preserve low-frequency heart sounds (S1/S2 "Lub-Dub").
Active Filtering: A 4th-order Sallen-Key Low-Pass Filter (Cutoff: $600Hz$) to remove high-frequency hiss, speech, and electromagnetic interference.
B. Digital Processing (The Logic Stage)
Controller: ESP32 DevKit V1 (Dual-core, 240MHz).
Signal Processing: Real-time Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) to convert time-domain audio into a Frequency Spectrogram.
Inference Engine: A TinyML Neural Network (trained via Edge Impulse) running on the ESP32. It analyzes the spectrogram 5 times per second to detect specific anomaly patterns (Murmurs/Arrhythmia).
C. User Interface (The Triage Stage)
Visual: 0.96" OLED Display showing a rolling Waveform and real-time BPM (Beats Per Minute).
Decision: "Traffic Light" LEDs for immediate triage:
🟢 Green: Normal Rhythm & Quality.
🔴 Red: Potential Murmur/Abnormality Detected (Refer to Doctor).
4. Key Innovations (USP)
5. Methodology (How It Works)
Placement: The device is placed on the patient's chest (Mitral/Tricuspid valve areas).
Acquisition: The Piezo sensor converts mechanical vibrations into a voltage signal.
Conditioning: The Analog circuit filters out noise above 600Hz and amplifies the signal to 3.3V range.
Digitization: The ESP32's ADC samples the audio at 4kHz.
Inference: The internal Neural Network classifies the audio clip.
Feedback: The OLED displays the heart rate, and the Red/Green LED lights up within 5 seconds to guide the health worker.
6. Feasibility & Tools
Hardware: All components (ESP32, TL072, Piezo) are standard and available in local electronics markets (SP Road/Ritchie Street).
Software Stack:
Firmware: Arduino IDE (C++).
AI Training: Edge Impulse (Web-based, Free Tier).
PCB Design: KiCad (Open Source - Critical for FOSSEE).
Enclosure: FreeCAD/OpenSCAD (Open Source).
7. Target Impact
Primary User: ASHA Workers, ANMs, and Rural Clinic Nurses.
Benefit: Reduces unnecessary referrals to city hospitals (cost saving) while ensuring critical cardiac cases are not missed (life-saving).
SDG Alignment: Directly targets UN SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being).
